Child Safety Standards

Standards Against Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE)

Last Updated: November 12, 2025

ZERO TOLERANCE POLICY FOR CHILD ABUSE AND EXPLOITATION

Only Care, operated by Innovfix Private Limited, is committed to protecting children and maintaining a platform that is completely free from child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). We have implemented comprehensive policies, procedures, and technical measures to prevent, detect, and respond to any potential CSAE content or activity.

1. Age Verification and Restrictions

STRICT AGE REQUIREMENT: Only Care is exclusively for users 18 years of age and older. Access by minors is strictly prohibited.

1.1 Age Verification Measures

  • Account Registration: Users must confirm they are 18+ during registration
  • Identity Verification: All support providers must submit government-issued photo ID for age verification
  • Document Verification: KYC (Know Your Customer) verification required for all creators/support providers
  • Automated Detection: Systems in place to detect and flag suspicious age-related information
  • Regular Audits: Ongoing reviews of user accounts for age compliance

1.2 Prevention of Minor Access

  • App store age ratings set to 18+ on all platforms
  • Age gates implemented at multiple points in user journey
  • Immediate account suspension for users found to be underage
  • Automated systems to detect minor-related keywords and content

2. Prohibited Content and Behavior

ABSOLUTELY PROHIBITED:

  • Any content depicting, promoting, or normalizing child sexual abuse or exploitation
  • Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) in any form
  • Grooming behavior or attempts to contact minors
  • Sexualized content involving minors or appearing to involve minors
  • Offering or requesting sexual content involving minors
  • Discussion or promotion of child exploitation
  • Links to external sites containing CSAM
  • Attempts to trade, share, or distribute CSAM

3. Detection and Monitoring Systems

3.1 Technical Safeguards

  • Content Moderation: All user-uploaded images undergo automated scanning
  • Hash Matching: Integration with known CSAM hash databases (PhotoDNA, others)
  • Keyword Detection: Automated systems flag suspicious keywords and phrases
  • Pattern Recognition: AI/ML models to detect grooming behavior patterns
  • Profile Monitoring: Automated review of profile information for concerning content
  • Session Monitoring: Systems in place to flag suspicious session patterns

3.2 Human Review

  • Dedicated moderation team for content review
  • Manual verification of all flagged content
  • Review of user reports within 24 hours
  • Escalation protocols for suspected CSAM
  • Regular training for moderation staff on CSAE identification

4. User Reporting Mechanisms

4.1 Easy Reporting Options

  • In-App Reporting: One-click reporting available on all profiles and content
  • Emergency Email: onlycare@innovfix.in for urgent safety concerns
  • 24/7 Monitoring: Safety team available around the clock
  • Anonymous Reporting: Users can report concerns without revealing identity
  • Multiple Report Categories: Specific option for child safety concerns

4.2 Report Response Time

  • Critical safety reports: Reviewed immediately (within 1 hour)
  • Suspected CSAM: Immediate action and law enforcement notification
  • Standard safety reports: Reviewed within 24 hours
  • Reporter notification: Updates provided on investigation status

5. Response and Enforcement Actions

5.1 Immediate Actions

Upon detection or report of potential CSAE content or behavior:

  • Content Removal: Immediate removal of violating content
  • Account Suspension: Instant suspension of involved accounts
  • Access Blocking: Permanent ban from platform
  • Evidence Preservation: Secure preservation of evidence for authorities
  • IP Blocking: Prevention of re-registration attempts

5.2 Law Enforcement Cooperation

MANDATORY REPORTING: We are committed to working with law enforcement and will:

  • Immediately report all suspected CSAM to appropriate authorities
  • Report to National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) as applicable
  • Report to local law enforcement authorities in India
  • Cooperate fully with investigations
  • Provide preserved evidence and user data as legally required
  • Maintain detailed logs for law enforcement requests

6. Reporting to Authorities

6.1 CyberTipline Reports (NCMEC)

  • All suspected CSAM reported to NCMEC CyberTipline (for US jurisdiction)
  • Reports include user information, IP addresses, timestamps, and content
  • Follow-up with law enforcement as requested

6.2 Indian Authorities

  • Cybercrime Reporting Portal: Reports filed with Indian government's cybercrime portal
  • Local Police: Cooperation with local law enforcement in Karnataka
  • NCRB (National Crime Records Bureau): Coordination as required

7. User Education and Awareness

7.1 Community Guidelines

  • Clear communication of child safety policies to all users
  • Mandatory acceptance of safety guidelines during onboarding
  • Regular reminders about reporting suspicious behavior
  • Safety tips prominently displayed in app

7.2 Support Provider Training

  • All support providers receive safety training before activation
  • Guidelines on recognizing and reporting concerning behavior
  • Mandatory review of child safety policies
  • Regular safety updates and refresher training

8. Platform Design for Safety

8.1 Safety-First Features

  • No Private Messaging for Non-Sessions: Communication limited to active sessions
  • Session Recording Metadata: All sessions logged with timestamps and participants
  • Profile Approval Process: All support provider profiles reviewed before going live
  • Limited Personal Information Sharing: Restrictions on sharing external contact information
  • Automated Blocking: Instant blocking of known violator accounts

8.2 Image Upload Restrictions

  • Profile photos undergo automated CSAM detection
  • Manual review of all uploaded images
  • Immediate rejection of inappropriate images
  • Hash matching against known CSAM databases

9. Third-Party Partnerships

9.1 Technology Partners

  • Integration with industry-standard CSAM detection tools
  • Partnership with content moderation service providers
  • Use of PhotoDNA or similar hash-matching technology
  • Regular updates to detection databases

9.2 Industry Collaboration

  • Participation in industry initiatives to combat CSAE
  • Sharing of best practices with other platforms
  • Regular review of emerging threats and countermeasures

10. Regular Audits and Updates

10.1 Policy Reviews

  • Quarterly review of child safety policies and procedures
  • Updates based on new threats and regulatory changes
  • External security audits annually
  • User feedback integration into safety improvements

10.2 Technology Updates

  • Continuous improvement of detection algorithms
  • Regular updates to content filtering systems
  • Integration of new safety technologies as they become available
  • Performance metrics tracking and optimization

11. Transparency and Accountability

11.1 Transparency Reports

  • Annual transparency reports on safety enforcement actions
  • Statistics on reports received and actions taken
  • Information on law enforcement cooperation (where legally permissible)

11.2 Contact Information

We maintain open communication channels for safety concerns and encourage reporting.

12. Commitment to Child Safety

Only Care pledges:

  • Zero tolerance for any form of child abuse or exploitation
  • Continuous investment in safety technologies and human moderation
  • Swift action against violators with permanent bans
  • Full cooperation with law enforcement investigations
  • Regular training for staff on CSAE identification and response
  • Transparency with users about our safety measures
  • Ongoing improvement of our safety systems and policies

13. Legal Compliance

Only Care complies with all applicable laws regarding child protection, including but not limited to:

  • India: Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012
  • India: Information Technology Act, 2000 and amendments
  • International: CRC (Convention on the Rights of the Child)
  • US: 18 U.S.C. ยง 2258A (CSAM reporting requirements) where applicable
  • All applicable international child protection laws
